5) DETAILED ANALYSIS

### vs. AWS Supply Chain
*   **Strength:** AWS dominates in data lake integration and broad ML capabilities.
*   **Weakness:** Generic models. Requires heavy customization to detect specific corridor drift.
*   **Fusion Advantage:** "Out of the box" drift detection specific to logistics corridors without requiring a data science team to build custom pipelines.

### vs. FourKites / project44
*   **Strength:** Massive carrier networks and ELD integrations. They own the "raw signal".
*   **Weakness:** They assume the signal is true. If a carrier spoofs GPS or an API drifts, they report it as fact.
*   **Fusion Advantage:** ChainIQ acts as a **Governance Layer**. We don't replace p44; we *audit* p44 by comparing their signal against our Shadow Models and Stability Indices.

### vs. Maersk NeoNav
*   **Strength:** Deep vertical integration for ocean freight.
*   **Weakness:** Carrier bias. Less effective for multi-carrier, agnostic orchestration.
*   **Fusion Advantage:** Neutrality. The Fusion Score is an unbiased mathematical assessment of risk, independent of the carrier's self-reporting.

7) SWOT ANALYSIS

**Strengths:**
*   Unique "Shadow Mode" methodology.
*   Low-latency (<45ms) allows inline API usage.
*   Agnostic to data source.

**Weaknesses:**
*   Lack of direct carrier network (dependent on upstream data).
*   New entrant (lack of historical baseline for some corridors).

**Opportunities:**
*   **Insurance:** Selling Fusion Scores to cargo insurers for dynamic premium adjustment.
*   **Finance:** Using Stability Index to underwrite supply chain finance (ChainPay).

**Threats:**
*   Incumbents building their own "Drift" detection.
*   Data providers cutting off access.

8) RECOMMENDATION
Position ChainIQ Fusion not as a competitor to p44/FourKites, but as a **complementary audit layer**. "Trust but Verify" is the sales motion.
